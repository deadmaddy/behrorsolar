PM SURYA GHAR & NET METERING

Common questions about solar subsidies in Behror & Neemrana

What is the PM Surya Ghar subsidy and who qualifies in Rajasthan?

PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana is a central government scheme that provides a direct subsidy on rooftop solar installations for residential households. In Rajasthan, eligible applicants are residential electricity consumers with a valid JVVNL, AVVNL, or JdVVNL connection. The system must be installed by a registered vendor and connected to the grid via net metering. Commercial and industrial connections are not eligible under this specific scheme.

How much subsidy can a household in Behror or Neemrana receive?

The subsidy is linked to the system size installed. For a 1–2 kW system the central subsidy is ₹30,000 per kW. For a 2–3 kW system it is ₹30,000 for the first 2 kW plus ₹18,000 for the third kW, capped at ₹78,000. Systems above 3 kW receive the same maximum of ₹78,000. The subsidy is credited directly to your bank account after successful net meter installation and inspection — not deducted upfront from the installer's bill.

Does PM Surya Ghar apply to rural households in Behror Rural and Neemrana Rural?

Yes — the scheme applies to both urban and rural residential consumers anywhere in Rajasthan, provided the household has an existing metered electricity connection from the state discom. Rural households in areas such as Behror Rural and Neemrana Rural served by AVVNL are eligible. If your property is on an agricultural or commercial connection rather than a domestic tariff, a separate assessment is needed before applying.

What is net metering and how does the approval process work in Rajasthan?

Net metering allows your rooftop solar system to export surplus electricity back to the grid. The discom installs a bidirectional meter that records both import and export. At billing time, exported units are deducted from units consumed — effectively spinning your meter backwards. In Rajasthan, the net metering application is submitted to the local discom after installation. Behror Solar handles the full application, technical inspection coordination, and follow-up with AVVNL on your behalf until the meter is active.

How long does a full installation take — from site survey to switch-on?

For a standard residential system in Behror or Neemrana, physical installation typically takes one to two days once the structure and materials are on site. The end-to-end timeline from initial site survey to net meter activation is usually three to five weeks, with most of that time spent on discom paperwork and inspection scheduling — not the installation itself. We keep you updated at each stage and manage the discom coordination directly so you do not have to follow up yourself.
