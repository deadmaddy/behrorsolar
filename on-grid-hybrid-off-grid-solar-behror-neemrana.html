On-Grid Solar

The most common choice in Behror & Neemrana

On-grid solar is the system most homeowners install — and for good reason. It is the simplest, lowest-cost configuration and the one fully supported by PM Surya Ghar and JVVNL net metering.

Your solar panels generate electricity during daylight hours. The inverter converts it to AC and powers your home first. Any surplus flows into the JVVNL grid — and that exported power is credited against your monthly bill under your net metering agreement. In practice, many households in Behror and Neemrana see their electricity bills drop to near zero or to only fixed charges after installation.

The one limitation: an on-grid system is legally required to shut down the moment the grid goes off. This is a safety requirement — without it, your inverter would push electricity into dead lines while JVVNL crews work on them. This is not a defect; it is how every standard grid-tied system works everywhere. If backup during outages matters to you, look at hybrid instead.

Typical Customer — Behror Urban

A homeowner with a monthly electricity bill of around ₹4,000–₹6,000 installs a 3–5 kW system. After net metering is activated by JVVNL, daytime consumption is largely offset by solar generation. Annual electricity expenses reduce significantly, and the PM Surya Ghar subsidy of up to ₹78,000 lowers the upfront cost further.

Hybrid Solar

Solar plus backup — for homes that cannot afford interruptions

A hybrid system adds a battery to the on-grid setup. The same solar panels generate electricity during the day. But instead of being entirely dependent on the grid, a hybrid inverter manages three sources simultaneously — panels, battery and grid — and decides how to use each one intelligently.

During normal daytime operation, panels power your home, charge the battery and export surplus to the grid — exactly like on-grid. The difference comes when the grid fails. A hybrid inverter detects the outage and instantly switches to island mode — disconnecting from the grid and running your home from panels and battery as a self-contained loop. Your fans, lights, Wi-Fi, refrigerator and CCTV keep running without interruption.

At night during an outage, the battery discharges to cover your essential loads. A typical 5 kWh battery can run fans, lights and internet for 8–10 hours depending on consumption.

Who needs this in Behror and Neemrana: families working from home, homes with medical equipment or security systems, businesses where even a few minutes of downtime has consequences, and rural households in Behror Rural or Neemrana Rural where 4–5 hours of daily load shedding is still the reality.

Typical Customer — Work-From-Home, Behror

A family with one member working from home needs uninterrupted internet, a laptop, lights and fans. A hybrid system with a 5 kWh battery provides seamless backup during any grid outage, while the solar panels continue reducing the electricity bill — the same financial benefit as on-grid, with added reliability.

Off-Grid Solar

When the grid is not an option

Off-grid solar is designed for properties where connection to the JVVNL grid is impractical, unreliable or simply unavailable. The system is entirely self-contained — panels generate power during daylight, a battery bank stores it, and an off-grid inverter manages the load independently. There is no grid connection at all.

This is not about escaping electricity bills. It is about having electricity where there is none — or where the connection is so unreliable that independence from the grid is the only practical solution.

We have completed over 800 off-grid installations across northeast Rajasthan — farmhouses, agricultural properties, remote rural homes and community projects. The battery sizing for an off-grid system is more critical than for hybrid because there is no grid to fall back on. The system must be designed to cover two to three days of cloudy weather without generation.

Typical Customer — Farmhouse, Rural Alwar District

A farmhouse without a dependable grid connection installs an off-grid system to power lighting, water pumps and household loads independently. Sized for the property's daily load with two days of battery backup, the system runs entirely on its own — no monthly electricity bill, no dependency on JVVNL reliability.

LOCAL CONTEXT — BEHROR & NEEMRANA

Power cuts in our service area — what you actually need to know

Grid reliability varies significantly between urban and rural areas in northeast Rajasthan. This affects which system makes most sense for your property.

Urban Behror & Urban Neemrana

Grid reliability has improved significantly in urban areas. Most residential connections now have a fairly stable supply, with outages mainly occurring due to:

  • Summer peak demand (May–June)
  • Storms and high winds
  • Scheduled maintenance
  • Local distribution faults

For most urban homeowners, the primary reason to install solar is reducing electricity bills and accessing the PM Surya Ghar subsidy — not backup power. An on-grid system is usually the right choice.

Rural Behror & Rural Neemrana

Rural areas see a different picture. Daily load shedding of 4–5 hours is still common, in addition to the same weather-related and maintenance outages as urban areas.

For a rural household, a hybrid system makes more sense — you get the bill reduction of solar generation and a battery that covers the daily outage window. An on-grid system alone would still leave you without power during the scheduled cuts.

Farmhouses and agricultural properties in these areas are often better served by an off-grid system entirely, removing dependence on grid reliability altogether.

Businesses & Commercial Properties

Even short outages — a few minutes — can disrupt operations, lose data, interrupt customer service or damage equipment.

For most businesses in Behror and Neemrana, hybrid is the recommendation regardless of urban or rural location. The battery cost is easily justified against the cost of even one significant operational interruption.

Will solar work during a power cut?

This is the most common question we hear from customers in Behror and Neemrana before they decide.

On-grid: No. Shuts down automatically when the grid fails. This is a legal safety requirement, not a product limitation.

Hybrid: Yes. Continues generating and switches to battery backup within milliseconds of a grid failure.

Off-grid: Not applicable. Never connected to the grid — outages do not affect it at all.

PM SURYA GHAR & SUBSIDIES

Which systems qualify for PM Surya Ghar in Rajasthan?

On-Grid — Fully Eligible

On-grid systems with JVVNL net metering are the primary systems PM Surya Ghar was designed for. Central subsidy of up to ₹78,000 plus the Rajasthan state subsidy of ₹17,000 apply. Subsidy is credited to your bank account after net meter installation.

Hybrid — Partially Eligible

The solar panel capacity in a hybrid system qualifies for PM Surya Ghar subsidy on the same terms as on-grid. The battery and hybrid inverter are not covered. Net metering with JVVNL is required — we handle this application on your behalf.

Off-Grid — Not Eligible

PM Surya Ghar requires a grid connection and net metering agreement. Off-grid systems, by definition, have neither. If your property can connect to the JVVNL grid, an on-grid or hybrid system would be eligible for subsidy.

Rajasthan State Subsidy — ₹17,000

Over and above the central government's ₹78,000, Rajasthan offers an additional ₹17,000 subsidy for eligible residential installations. Most customers are unaware of this. Combined, the total available subsidy is ₹95,000. Ask us to confirm current availability at the time of your application.

COMMON QUESTIONS

Questions we hear every day from customers in Behror & Neemrana

Will my solar system work during a power cut in Behror or Neemrana?

It depends on which system you install. An on-grid system shuts down automatically when the grid fails — this is a legal safety requirement to protect JVVNL linemen working on the lines. A hybrid system continues working during outages, switching to battery backup within milliseconds. An off-grid system is unaffected by grid outages entirely since it has no grid connection. If backup during power cuts is important to you, hybrid is the right choice for urban areas; for rural properties with daily load shedding, hybrid or off-grid depending on whether a grid connection exists.

How much roof space do I need for solar in Behror or Neemrana?

A rough guide: each kW of solar capacity requires approximately 80–100 sq ft of shadow-free roof area. A 3 kW system needs around 250 sq ft, a 5 kW system around 400 sq ft, and a 10 kW system around 800 sq ft. These are averages — actual area depends on panel efficiency, roof orientation and shading from nearby structures. We assess this during the free site survey before quoting, so you will get an accurate figure for your specific property.

Which solar system qualifies for PM Surya Ghar subsidy in Rajasthan?

On-grid systems and the solar panel component of hybrid systems qualify for PM Surya Ghar subsidy. Off-grid systems do not, as the scheme requires a JVVNL net metering connection. The central government subsidy is ₹30,000 per kW for the first 2 kW and ₹18,000 for the third kW, capped at ₹78,000 for systems of 3 kW and above. Rajasthan also provides an additional ₹17,000 state subsidy, bringing the total available to ₹95,000 for eligible residential installations.

How long does net metering take with JVVNL in Behror and Neemrana?

Physical installation of your solar system typically takes one to two days. The end-to-end timeline from site survey to net meter activation by JVVNL is usually three to five weeks, with most of that time spent on documentation, inspection scheduling and JVVNL processing. We submit the net metering application on your behalf and follow up with JVVNL directly — you do not need to manage this yourself.

How much can I realistically save with rooftop solar in Behror or Neemrana?

Savings depend on your current electricity consumption, system size and how much of your usage happens during daylight hours. As a practical reference from our installed customer base: homeowners consuming 500 units per month typically see their bills drop to near zero after a 3 kW installation with net metering. Customers using 700 units per month with a 5 kW system report similar outcomes. The savings are highest in summer months when both consumption and solar generation peak simultaneously.
