DCR vs NON-DCR — WHAT IT MEANS FOR YOU

The panel you choose determines whether you get the subsidy

This is the most important decision a homeowner in Behror or Neemrana needs to make before buying solar.

DCR — Domestic Content Requirement

Made in India — Subsidy Eligible

DCR panels are manufactured in India and certified under the PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana. Every panel comes with a government-issued DCR certificate confirming where it was made — from wafer to cell to finished module. For homeowners in Behror and Neemrana, DCR certification is the single most important factor in accessing the full ₹95,000 subsidy.

  • Eligible for ₹78,000 central government subsidy
  • Eligible for ₹17,000 Rajasthan state subsidy
  • Total subsidy: up to ₹95,000
  • Required for JVVNL net metering under PM Surya Ghar
  • Certificate traceable from raw material to finished panel
Non-DCR — Imported or Non-Certified

High Performance — No Subsidy

Non-DCR panels are typically imported or manufactured outside the DCR certification chain. They often offer higher raw efficiency and lower per-watt cost, but they do not qualify for PM Surya Ghar subsidy. A customer choosing non-DCR panels pays the full system cost with no government support.

  • No PM Surya Ghar subsidy eligibility
  • No Rajasthan state subsidy
  • Suitable for commercial, industrial and off-grid use
  • Often higher efficiency per watt
  • Lower upfront panel cost in many cases
PANEL TECHNOLOGY

Mono PERC, TOPCon and HJT — what is the difference?

All three technologies convert sunlight into electricity. The difference is in how efficiently they do it and how they perform in Rajasthan's heat.

Technology Typical Efficiency Heat Performance Low-Light Output Cost Availability
Mono PERC 19% – 23.3% Good Good Lowest Wide
TOPCon 21% – 24% Better Better Moderate Wide
HJT 23% – 25% Best Best Highest Limited

For rooftops in Behror and Neemrana where temperatures regularly exceed 40°C in summer, TOPCon's better heat coefficient translates to measurably higher generation during peak summer months — precisely when your air conditioner load is highest and generation matters most.

SOLAR PANEL FAQ

Common questions about solar panels in Behror & Neemrana

What is a DCR panel and why does it matter for PM Surya Ghar?

DCR stands for Domestic Content Requirement. A DCR panel is manufactured entirely in India — from wafer to cell to finished module — and carries a government-issued certificate confirming this. The PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana requires DCR-certified panels for subsidy eligibility. If you install a non-DCR panel, you lose the ₹78,000 central subsidy and ₹17,000 Rajasthan state subsidy. For a residential homeowner in Behror or Neemrana, that is ₹95,000 of financial benefit that depends entirely on the panel you choose.

Which DCR panel has the highest efficiency available?

Based on government-issued DCR certificates, Tata Solar's TP590LG10B Mono PERC panel at 23.3% efficiency is the highest available DCR-certified panel in the current market. This is verified by Tata's DCR certificate issued under PM Surya Ghar, showing manufacture by TP Solar Limited. Premier Energies TOPCon DCR follows at 22.58%, and Adani TOPCon DCR at 22.5%. UTL's Mono PERC DCR panel is certified at 20.35%.

Can I use non-DCR panels and still get the PM Surya Ghar subsidy?

No. PM Surya Ghar subsidy is only available for systems installed with DCR-certified panels. JVVNL verifies panel certificates during the net metering inspection. If non-DCR panels are found, the subsidy application is rejected. Any installer who promises PM Surya Ghar subsidy with non-DCR panels is either misinformed or misrepresenting the scheme. Always ask for the DCR certificate before committing to an installation.

What is the difference between Mono PERC and TOPCon solar panels?

Both are monocrystalline silicon technologies but TOPCon adds a thin tunnel oxide layer that passivates the cell surface and reduces recombination losses. The practical result is that TOPCon panels produce slightly more electricity per watt of rated capacity, perform better in high temperatures — relevant for Rajasthan summers — and have a lower degradation rate over the panel's 25-year life. Tata's Mono PERC DCR at 23.3% efficiency is an exception — it outperforms most DCR TOPCon panels currently available because of TP Solar's manufacturing quality, not because PERC is inherently better than TOPCon.
