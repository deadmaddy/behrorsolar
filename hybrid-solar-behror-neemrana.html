HOW HYBRID SOLAR WORKS

Three situations, one system that handles all of them

This is what makes hybrid solar fundamentally different from standard on-grid solar.

Daytime — Grid Available

Panels power your home, charge your battery and export surplus

When the grid is running and the sun is out, your 3 kW TOPCon panels generate DC electricity. The Midea hybrid inverter converts it to AC and prioritises your home's loads first. Surplus power charges the Voltra battery. Once the battery is full, any remaining surplus exports to the JVVNL grid under your net metering agreement — reducing your bill further.

☀️ Panels→ ⚡ Inverter→ 🏠 Home loads→ 🔋 Battery charges→ 🔌 Export to grid
Daytime — Grid Outage (Power Cut)

Why standard solar stops — and why hybrid does not

A standard on-grid solar system is legally required to shut down the moment the grid goes down. This is an electrical safety requirement — if your inverter kept feeding power into a dead grid, it could electrocute linemen working on the lines. So during every power cut, your standard solar system goes completely offline — even with full sun.

A hybrid inverter solves this with automatic islanding. When it detects the grid has gone down, it instantly disconnects from the grid and forms a self-contained local power supply — your panels, inverter and battery working as a closed loop. Your appliances do not even notice the switchover. Panels continue generating, battery continues charging, your home keeps running.

☀️ Panels→ ⚡ Inverter (islanded)→ 🏠 Home loads→ 🔋 Battery charges
Grid is disconnected. No export. No risk to linemen. Your home runs independently.
Night — Grid Outage

The battery takes over — hours of backup with no panels needed

The Voltra 5.2 kWh battery charged throughout the day. At night, when the grid is out and there is no sun, the inverter draws from the battery to power your home. At a typical household load of 500W — fans, lights, phone charging — the 5.2 kWh battery provides around 8–10 hours of backup. Running heavier loads will reduce this, but essential circuits can run through the night.

The Battery Management System (BMS) monitors cell health and prevents over-discharge, so you get the full rated 3,000+ cycle life from the Voltra battery.

🔋 Battery discharges→ ⚡ Inverter→ 🏠 Home loads (night)
No sun required. Battery alone runs your home through the night.

HYBRID SOLAR — FAQ

Common questions about hybrid solar in Behror & Neemrana

Can I get PM Surya Ghar subsidy on a hybrid system?

The PM Surya Ghar subsidy applies to the solar panel capacity — ₹30,000 per kW up to 2 kW, capped at ₹78,000 for 3 kW and above. The battery and hybrid inverter are not covered by the central subsidy, but the panels portion of a hybrid system is fully eligible. Net metering must still be applied for via JVVNL, which we handle on your behalf.

How long will the 5.2 kWh Voltra battery run my home during a power cut?

At a typical nighttime load of 400–500W — fans, lights, phone charging — the 5.2 kWh battery provides 8–10 hours of backup. Running a refrigerator alongside reduces this to around 6–8 hours. Running a 1.5-ton AC significantly reduces backup time to 2–3 hours. We recommend identifying which circuits matter most during outages.

Why does standard on-grid solar stop working during a power cut?

Standard on-grid inverters are legally required to shut down when the grid fails to prevent backfeeding electricity into dead lines, which could electrocute linemen. A hybrid inverter solves this with automatic islanding — it disconnects from the grid and continues powering your home from panels and battery independently.

How many years will the Voltra LiFePO₄ battery last?

The Voltra BESS is rated for 3,000+ charge cycles at 80% depth of discharge. At one cycle per day that is over 8 years. In practice, the real service life is typically 10–12 years. LiFePO₄ chemistry degrades more slowly than other lithium types and is not sensitive to partial charging.

Is a 3 kW hybrid system suitable for my home in Behror or Neemrana?

A 3 kW hybrid system suits homes consuming 300–600 units per month with around 250–300 sq ft of usable roof area. Both Behror and Neemrana receive excellent solar irradiance throughout the year. We do a free site survey before any quote to confirm suitability for your specific property.
